Shape

  • Male : The plumage is iridescent green, the tail is very long, copper-green, each feather at the tip has a star of green-green, red copper, yellow, brown. The crest is long, narrow and vertical, its face is yellow and green, when it dances its tail spreads out into a fan shape to attract female birds.
  • Female : Almost like a male, but without a crest, without decorative feathers. The tail feathers of the female are usually short and have a brown border. Dark brown eyes, horny gray beak, gray legs.

    • Ration

    Peacocks are omnivores and eat mainly plants, petals, seed heads, insects and other arthropods , reptiles and amphibians . Wild peacocks forage by raking the leaves early in the morning or at dusk. They retreat into the shade and safety of the woods during the hottest part of the day. Peacocks are not picky eaters, will eat almost anything they can fit into their beaks and digest. Birds actively hunt insects such as ants, crickets and termites; rolling mat; arthropods and other small mammals. Indian peacocks also eat small snakes.

    Domesticated peacocks may also eat bread and loose grains such as oats and corn, cheese, cooked rice and sometimes cat food. Breeders have found that peacocks prefer high-protein foods including barn -destroying larvae , various meats and fruits, as well as vegetables including dark leafy greens, broccoli, carrots, and beans , beets and peas.
