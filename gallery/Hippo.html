Overview

Hippos are semi-aquatic, semi-terrestrial, inhabiting the rivers , lakes and mangrove swamps of West Africa where males dominate a stretch of river and lead herds of 5 to 30 females and calves. young. During the day, they maintain their cool by soaking in water or mud; and spawning also takes place in water. They come ashore at night to eat grass. Although hippos rest close together in the water, foraging is a solitary and non-territorial activity.

Despite their physical resemblance to pigs and other terrestrial ungulates, they are most closely related to whales and dolphins , which diverged about 55 million years ago. The common ancestor of whales and hippos diverged from other even-toed ungulates about 60 million years ago. The earliest known hippo fossils belong to the African genus Kenyapotamus , dating to about 16 million years ago.

Hippos are recognized by their round, almost hairless body, large mouth and jaws, short legs, and large size. They are the third largest land mammal in terms of weight (1½ to 3 tons), after the white rhinoceros (1½ to 3½ tons) and three species of elephants (3 to 9 tons), 3.6–4 m long, tall from 1.5-1.7m. Hippos are one of the largest four-legged animals . Despite having short legs and a stocky build, they can easily run faster than humans. They have been recorded at speeds of 30 km/h (19 mph) over short distances. Hippos are one of the most aggressive creatures in the world and are often considered one of the most dangerous animals in Africa. There are currently an estimated 125,000 to 150,000 hippos throughout sub-Saharan Africa ; Zambia (40,000) and Tanzania (20,000–30,000) are the two countries with the largest hippo populations. They are still threatened by habitat loss and poaching for their meat and ivory fangs .
