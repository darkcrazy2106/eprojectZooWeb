Evolution

Dolphins, like whales, are descendants of terrestrial mammals , and may all belong to the phylum Artiodactyl . The ancestors of today's modern dolphins may have entered the water about 50 million years ago during the Eocene epoch .

Modern dolphin bones have two small , rod-shaped pelvises and are given atrophied hind legs. October 2006, a strange bottlenose dolphin was caught in Japan; This fish has 2 small fins on either side of the genital slit and is determined by scientists to have developed hind legs.

Senses

Most dolphins have keen eyesight both in their lips and in the water and can perceive frequencies 10 times higher than humans can hear. Although dolphins have small ears on either side of their heads, it is thought that in aquatic environments, fish hear with their lower jaws and conduct sound to the middle ear through openings in the jawbone. Hearing is also used to broadcast biological radar, an ability all dolphins have. It is thought that dolphin teeth are used as receptors, they receive incoming sounds and indicate the exact position of the subject. Dolphin's sense of touch is also well-developed, with nerve endings densely distributed over the skin , especially in the nose ., pectoral fins and genital area. However, dolphins do not have smell receptors and are therefore believed to have no sense of smell . Dolphins also have taste buds and show a preference for certain fish foods. Dolphins spend most of their time underwater, sensing the taste of water can help dolphins smell in the same way that taste can tell fish the presence of objects beyond their mouth.

Although dolphins have no hair, they still have hair follicles that help perform some tactile functions. It is believed that the tiny hairs on the beak of the Boto river dolphin act as a tactile system to compensate for the species' poor eyesight .
